## CV Title Strategy (Founder Syndrome)

### The Problem
Founder syndrome: employees at your own startup get "Senior Engineer at Genera" on their CV and become more hireable, while "CTO & Co-founder" of an unknown company reads as "small startup, did everything, unclear depth." Recruiters and ATS parse titles literally.

### Recommended Title Alternatives (by context)

**Genera:**
- For engineering roles: Keep "CTO & Co-founder" but ensure bullets read like a senior engineer's output (system design, architecture decisions, scale metrics)
- For startup/EIR roles: "CTO & Co-founder" is an asset, keep as-is
- Alternative if CTO hurts: "Senior Software Engineer & Co-founder" or "Founding Engineer"

**Toke:**
- CV title: "Senior Fullstack Engineer -> Tech Lead" (defensible: he was de facto CTO making all technology decisions)
- Never: "Consultant" or "Developer" (undersells scope)

**Freelance:**
- CV title: "Lead Fullstack Engineer" (sole technical person on most projects)

**Keepers:**
- "Tech Lead" is already strong, keep as-is

**404 Tech Found:**
- Keep "Founder & Tech Lead" for all contexts (leadership + technical)

### Two-CV Strategy
Consider maintaining two versions:
1. **Founder-framed:** For startup roles, EIR, grants, fellowships. Lead with CTO/Founder titles.
2. **Engineer-framed:** For developer/IC roles. Lead with Senior Engineer/Tech Lead titles. Describe the same work through an IC lens (architecture, systems, scale).
